Case Studies

The following are composite illustrations based on representative engagement types. All details are fictionalized.

Motion Practice

Summary Judgment Brief Delivered Under a Collapsed Timeline

A solo practitioner handling a commercial contract dispute in federal court lost her associate to an emergency family leave two weeks before the summary judgment deadline. She had the arguments mapped, the record assembled, and the strategy set — but no bandwidth to execute the drafting.

CourtReady took her outline, deposition excerpts, and annotated case law and produced a complete draft memorandum of law — statement of facts, legal standard, argument sections, and conclusion — within 72 hours. Two rounds of revision followed, incorporating counsel's strategic edits and jurisdictional formatting requirements for the Southern District of New York. The final work product was delivered in clean, editable Word format five days before the filing deadline.

Counsel filed on time, without compromising the quality of the argument or pulling late nights.

Appellate

Appellate Brief Structured Across a 280-Page Record

A litigation boutique handling a mid-size appellate matter had the legal arguments well-developed but faced a structural problem: the record was sprawling, the exhibits were inconsistently labeled, and the appendix needed to be court-ready under strict Second Circuit formatting rules. The partner didn't need another lawyer — she needed someone to impose order on the volume.

CourtReady handled the appendix organization from the ground up: inventoried all record materials, applied consistent tab and labeling conventions, cross-referenced citations throughout the brief draft, and assembled the final appendix package to circuit spec. Simultaneously, CourtReady structured the argument sections of the brief against the issues on appeal, producing a draft the partner could work from directly.

The firm submitted a clean, professionally organized brief on deadline. The partner noted it was the first appellate matter in two years where the appendix required no last-minute scrambling.

Bundle Assembly

Multi-Party Filing Package Coordinated Across Three Workstreams

A mid-size firm representing a defendant in a complex commercial dispute needed to file coordinated motion papers — a primary brief, two supporting affidavits, and a 40-exhibit appendix — across a tight window. Three attorneys were contributing materials from separate workstreams, and no one had ownership of pulling it together into a single, court-compliant package.

CourtReady stepped in as the assembly layer: collected all materials from the three contributors, formatted each document to New York Supreme Court Commercial Division standards, sequenced and labeled all exhibits with a complete table of contents, ran a pre-submission filing checklist against local rules, and identified two formatting gaps that would have caused a rejection at the clerk's window.

The complete bundle was delivered 48 hours before the filing deadline, requiring only counsel's signature and review before submission.
